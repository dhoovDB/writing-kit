# PROBLEM

*Don't skip this section. A solution without a proven problem is just a guess.*

> **Delegation:** This is the section to write yourself before involving AI.
> Your judgment about the customer and the problem is the input — not something
> to generate. Use AI to sharpen the language once you know what you think.

## Who has the problem

*Name the customer. Put them in a real situation. Not "users" — a specific person
doing a specific thing.*

## What the problem is

*What are they trying to do? What's in the way? Write this from their perspective.*

**Prompt:** "Today, [customer] has to [painful thing]. This means [consequence for them]."

## Why it matters

*Quantify it. Large abstract number? Add a comparison that makes it real.*

## Hypothesis

*Your test-and-learn bet, stated explicitly:*

> We believe [customer] experiences [problem]. If we solve it by [approach],
> we expect [measurable outcome]. We'll know we're right when [signal].
